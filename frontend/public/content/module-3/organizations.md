## 🏢 Organizations — A Home for Big Projects

Big open source projects like React, Django, or Kubernetes aren't owned by a single person. They live under **GitHub Organizations**.

Think of an Organization like an **apartment building** for projects. Instead of one person owning the whole thing, a group of people share responsibility. They manage the building together, assign apartments (repositories) to teams, and set rules for who can access what.

## 💼 Why Do Organizations Exist?

Organizations make it easier to manage large projects by offering:

- **🤝 Shared Ownership**: Multiple people can manage settings, billing, and permissions together.
- **👥 Teams**: Contributors can be grouped into teams like `@react/core-team` or `@react/docs-team`. This makes it easy to notify the right people for reviews.
- **🔑 Fine-Grained Permissions**: You can give read access to everyone, write access to the triage team, and admin access to core maintainers — all with a few clicks.

### 🧠 Key Takeaway

Organizations are shared accounts that let teams manage projects together. Most major open source projects live inside organizations, not under individual people's accounts.
